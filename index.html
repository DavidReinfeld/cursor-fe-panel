SECTION 03

10/20/30 Day Impact Plan

Days 1–10

Learn, embed, and take ownership

The Business and Deals

Understand goals, pipeline health, where deals are stalling, coverage/capacity model, and where revenue is at risk. I’ll find our 2–3 highest stakes deals and embed in them. Pull the top 20 accounts by PLG seat concentration and stack-rank them by expansion readiness. Own a live discovery or demo by EOW. If there are aligned sellers that need FE support without capacity, I’m owning these engagements.

Hiring and Recruiting

Take immediate ownership of open reqs and pipeline from Sahil. Partner with recruiting to understand process and identify any gaps to be solved. Outbound candidate PG immediately. Categorize candidate quality from 1–3 based on criteria discussed earlier.

Product, Personas and PoV

Ramp by doing. Use Cursor on real tasks, watch workshops, join customer calls, talk to 2 existing customer champions, build an agent to scan existing calls to surface ICP patterns and CoM framework. Learn what customers want that we don’t have today — by my vertical specifically. Are we selling with an industry PoV or are we selling features?

Team

Start with direct team, immediate peers/partners, and top 3 highest impact/tenure FEs. Learn what’s driving success and failure in hiring and performance. For directs, share intake form, hold 1:1s, understand them as people, where they need support, what’s slowing them down, and where I need to learn on them. Understand team norms and map the gap against the 4 key areas for success (values, competencies, op. rhythm, career growth).

Days 11–20

Credibility, experiments, and wins

Establishing Credibility

Team…

Show up with humility, ready to roll up my sleeves, learn, and ask the stupid questions out loud to create a low-ego learning environment. Demonstrate deep care for the wellbeing, performance, and career trajectory of my team as well as strong fundamentals on how to do it without creating chaos. Share a thoughtful and honest PoV on the current state of the team (headwinds and tailwinds).

XFN (Sales)…

Show up ready to learn, understand their needs and approach, and personally get hands-on early. Demonstrate strong understanding of the business, CoM/sales fundamentals, and provide transparency around team standards they can expect. Meet with customers right away.

Customers…

Be transparent that I won’t be a Cursor expert yet — but show up as a thought partner by understanding their problems, having a PoV on the industry/vertical landscape and how to evaluate our technology, and sharing where we see the category heading. Ask questions that demonstrate I’m being thoughtful about their world and pains. Customers are looking for partners to help them navigate a rapidly changing LLM quality, platform capability, and cost landscape.

Initial Changes and Experiments

The business is moving from PLG → SLG and is also focusing on verticals. The 3 following areas are those we can get quick experimental wins in:

Do we have the right discovery and qualification framework as well as POC process to run controlled cycles? Do AEs and SCs agree on what those are? Are we documenting required capabilities as a shared artifact with the customer?

Are we selling with an industry specific PoV or a general one? Leading with features, or narrative? Are our “pitches” resulting in low value deals or transformative engagements?

Are the proposed team level values, competencies, and rhythm I discussed earlier congruent with the reality and current state of the business? What changes should we consider to adapt them?

Early Wins

  1. 01POC framework live in one active deal. AE and FE aligned on success criteria. Customer has signed off on required capabilities as a shared artifact. An agent is helping us build this documentation.
  2. 02Vertical PoV tested in market against a feature-led pitch in a comparable account. We have a directional read on which one moves enterprise buyers faster.
  3. 03ICP patterns and top unmet needs by vertical delivered to PM as a structured artifact. The feedback loop has an owner, a cadence, and a home.
  4. 04Two exceptional FE candidates in process. At least one sourced through outbound, not inbound.
  5. 05Every direct has had a substantive 1:1 and I have a clear view of who’s operating at ceiling, who has untapped capacity, and where I need to develop or backfill. The team has a shared understanding of what exceptional looks like and why the bar is where it is.
  6. 06I’m personally supporting top deals for unsupported sellers. Coverage doesn’t stop if I haven’t hired.

Days 21–30

Impact and measurement

What does meaningful impact look like? What systems, processes, and norms are now in place?

The team feels like a team. Clear values, high standards, and a leader who knows them and can help them get to where they want to go

We deeply understand our business and how to win the vertical. Our product gaps, industry PoV, and unique process based on the nuance of our industry ICP.

We have a clear path to partnering with key members of the product team. Systematic feedback sharing and communication cadences to ensure we’re positioned to deliver on expectations of vertical specific needs.

Sales views FE as a high performing competitive advantage in deals, not a support function. We have alignment on standards, operating rhythm, communication styles, and expectations.

We’ve up-leveled how we show up in technical validation and customer engagements. Documentation, rigor, and follow-through set the standard — customers reciprocate the effort and choose us over the competition because we’re the best partner, not just the best product.

Agents are in place to accelerate learning. We can’t be in all places at once but agents can. We now have real-time insights into what messaging is landing and what new stories/insights we can incorporate into our discussions.

How do you measure success at this stage?

Acceptance of a New Business Meeting with EB attendance and structured, timebound POCs started. Have we told a compelling story and showed up as credible thought partners where developers want to meaningfully engage?

Cases of quantifiable pain and negative consequences documented. Are we going deeper on business and contextual discovery as we’re conducting technical discovery?

FE influence on deal size and outcomes. Are we telling a bigger, more differentiated story or just selling features?

Candidate pipeline quality. Have we increased the number of 2+ and 3 level candidates considering Cursor?

Team retention and engagement. Does the team feel supported and optimistic about the future?
